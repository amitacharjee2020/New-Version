RFID is a technology that uses radio frequencies to identify objects in real time. You’ll find RFID used in everything from passports and license plates to inventory tracking systems and electronic toll collection. It’s also an increasingly popular way to track objects in supply chain management and logistics. RFID is really just a form of barcoding, but it’s faster and more precise than traditional barcode scanning. It allows you to read much smaller tags—as small as 1mm—and scan them from farther away, which makes it ideal for use in large warehouses where there are many obstructions between the reader and tag. RFID is also more secure than barcodes because it doesn’t require line-of-sight between reader and tag. That means you can use it to prevent theft of items like clothing or electronics by disabling their tags when they’re removed from store shelves but still keeping track of inventory as it moves through your warehouse or distribution network.
